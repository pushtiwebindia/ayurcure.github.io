Ayurvedic Treatment for Cancer -  Cancer Treatment through Ayurveda, Ayurvedic Medicine for Cancer

Cancer is of various type and even divided into various groups like malignant and non malignant. According to stage, some divided into 3 stages, some divided into 6 stages. In case of cancer, there is multiplications of cells which results in the cancerous growth; slowly it infects adjacent cells. After some time, i.e. after 1st and 2nd stage, in which cancer starts at a particular part of the body, metastasis takes place, in which cancerous cells move from one part of the body to another and then it leads into the advanced stage. Once disease reach into advanced stage, it becomes difficult to treat the disease.

The University of Texas M.D. Anderson's cancer centre website reports that five to ten percentages of cancer cases can come from person's genetic make-up. Cancer cure depends on disease stage. Earlier stage i.e. 1st and 2nd is curable but after that when disease advanced into 3rd -4th stages, then it becomes tough to cure the disease. In 1st stage, cancer starts at a particular part of body and shows mild or no symptoms and then it slowly occupies the adjacent part of body and reach into 2nd stage. After this, metastasis takes place and cancerous cells starts growing at other parts of the body and reach to stage 3rd and 4th. These stages i.e. 3rd and 4th are advanced stages of the disease.

Cancer is a class of diseases characterized by out-of-control cell growth. There are over 100 different types of cancer, and each is classified by the type of cell that is initially affected.
Cancer harms the body when damaged cells divide uncontrollably to form lumps or masses of tissue called tumors (except in the case of leukemia where cancer prohibits normal blood function by abnormal cell division in the blood stream). Tumors can grow and interfere with the digestive, nervous, and circulatory systems, and they can release hormones that alter body function. Tumors that stay in one spot and demonstrate limited growth are generally considered to be benign.Cause of Cancer - Cancer is ultimately the result of cells that uncontrollably grow and do not die. Normal cells in the body follow an orderly path of growth, division, and death. Programmed cell death is called apoptosis, and when this process breaks down, cancer begins to form. Unlike regular cells, cancer cells do not experience programmatic death and instead continue to grow and divide. This leads to a mass of abnormal cells that grows out of control."
